Social Security Disability Overview



At American Disability Solutions, our Examiners and Associates have extensive experience in dealing with Social Security’s maze of procedures, technicalities and law. We provide easy and unparalleled access to our examiners and representatives. We contact our clients regularly to provide updates as they are available and we are constantly researching local and state public assistance programs that are available to aid our clients while they are waiting for the Social Security Administration to process their claim. Please allow us to work for you in obtaining the benefits that you deserve.

It is important to note that one of the biggest complaints about the Social Security disability application process is that it takes too long. Our examiners and representatives are constantly learning new and innovative ways to expedite the Social Security disability application process. In some cases, applications can be evaluated in a timely manner. We will work hand-in-hand with you to carefully consider whether your case qualifies for an “on the record decision”.

We will fight for you from your initial application through your hearing. American Disability Solutions represents individuals at all of the following levels.


Initial Application

All SSDI and SSI claims begin by filing an initial application. Your application is then submitted to the local Disability Determination Services (DDS) office to be reviewed and evaluated by a disability adjudicator and physician or psychologist. If the medical records that Social Security has access to are inadequate, you may be asked to take a special examination called a “consultative examination.” Unfortunately, almost every initial application can be expected to be denied at first. However, do not be discouraged as you have every right to challenge an adverse determination and we will help you. If your initial application is denied, you must file an appeal within sixty (60) days or you will lose your right to appeal and your latest decision will become final.


Reconsideration

Your first appeal is to request “reconsideration” of your initial application. Most states require this step of the process; however, some states have eliminated this step completely. “Reconsideration” is a complete review of your claim by someone who did not participate in the initial decision. That person is supposed to review all of the original evidence used to make the initial decision, plus any additional evidence which may be provided with the reconsideration process. If your reconsideration is denied, you must file an appeal within sixty (60) days or you will lose your right to appeal and your latest decision will become final.


Hearing

The next stage in the appeals process is to request a hearing before an Administrative Law Judge (ALJ). An ALJ is an individual who works for the Social Security Administration. The ALJ will decide on the time and place for your hearing. You and your attorney, examiner and representatives will receive a notice of a hearing with this information. The hearing itself is quite informal; nevertheless it is a legal proceeding. Attendees will include the ALJ, an assistant and you and your attorney or representative. In some cases there will also be a vocational expert and/or medical expert to testify. There is no Jury. The hearing will be electronically recorded.

We provide representation at the Hearing before an Administrative Law Judge level. If you lose this stage of the process, you have the right to appeal to the Appeals Council We are not agreeing to undertake this next stage of the process, should it become necessary.
