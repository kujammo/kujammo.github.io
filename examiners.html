Social Security Disability Examiners



Thank you for retaining American Disability Solutions regarding your Social Security Disability claim. I appreciate the confidence you have placed in my firm and we look forward to working with you on your Social Security disability claim. I understand that this may be an overwhelming process. However, the documentation in this packet is designed to help build your case. We will be collecting many pieces of information, including medical records, consultations, evaluations, emergency room reports, and opinions from your treating medical providers.

If you would like us to represent you, please make sure that the following documents are completed and signed by you:


1. Client Questionnaire

Please fill out this document as completely as possible. If you do not have the specific information requested, please state “unknown” rather than simply leave it blank. A Medical Provider List is within this Questionnaire. Please complete this list to the best of your ability pertaining to your medical treatment and medication usage. This will allow us to obtain the medical records necessary to support your potential claims. After fully completing the client questionnaire, please sign and date the form.


2. Form 1696 ( 2 copies )

By signing this document, you are appointing American Disability Solutions to represent you in the Social Security Disability Claims Process.


3. Fee Agreement for Legal Services

This agreement sets forth the basis upon which American Disability Solutions will undertake to represent you concerning your potential claim(s). Please note that you do not owe us any fees or expenses if we are unsuccessful in obtaining approval of your claim. Please review sign and date this agreement.

Additionally, in order to obtain the medical records, related to your claim, we need medical authorizations signed by you. Please sign where indicated on both of the documents titled, Authorizations for Release of Personal Health Information.


If we are unable to assist you in applying during your intake appointment, the next thing that you will need to do is call the Social Security Administration and setup an appointment to complete an application for Social Security disability benefits. This is either because this is your first application for disability or because it has been longer than 60 days since you were last denied and you passed the allowed timeframe for an appeal. You can schedule your application interview to be conducted in person or by telephone. We have included a Contact Form for Your local SSA Field Office. This form provides you with the address and phone number of your local field office where you will go to complete your initial application for the Social Security Disability Benefits. You can also choose to go online at www.ssa.gov. to fill out your application instead of in person or by telephone.


1. List all medical information

This list should include names, addresses, telephone numbers, fax numbers of all previous and current doctors as well as dates and locations of doctor’s visits and hospitalization. This should also include dates and locations of x-rays, CT scans, MRI’s and other clinical diagnostics that provide evidence of your conditions.


2. List all prescribed medications and current dosages

This list should include the names of all medications you currently take, the dosage, and how often you take them as well as what physician prescribed the medication.


3. List your previous employers for the last 15 years

The interviewer will need the employer’s name, the job description of the positions held and the approximate length of your employment of each job you have had in those last 15 years. Make sure that you tell your interviewer the most that you did at those jobs such as the most weight lifted and the longest that you stood on your feet. You should bring your most recent W-2 forms with you as well.


4. Other

The interviewer may ask for documentation such as a photo I.D., Social Security Card, Marriage/Divorce Certificates, Birth/Death Certificates, Military Service or Discharge paperwork and information on Child Support payments.


Please remember to be open and honest with the Interviewer when giving them the information about your medical conditions that prevent you from working a full time job. Many claimants fail to discuss certain impairments such as learning disabilities, problems with concentration and memory and symptoms from mental conditions, such as depression or anxiety, that contribute to precluding full time employment. If you suffer from any of these conditions, make sure that you mention them to the to the Interviewer when you apply for Social Security, please call us and let us know immediately so that we may start the appeals process if you are denied.
